## Abstract

High dynamic range (HDR) image reconstruction has traditionally relied on task-specific architectures trained on bracketed exposure sequences. In this work, we investigate whether a foundation model pre-trained with a self-supervised objective on large-scale multi-exposure data can serve as a general-purpose encoder for HDR-related tasks.

We propose an exposure-contrastive pre-training strategy and evaluate the resulting representations on three downstream tasks: HDR reconstruction from a single image, tone mapping quality prediction, and low-light object detection.

Results indicate that the pre-trained encoder provides consistent improvements across all three tasks, with particularly strong transfer in the low-data regime.

<KeyPoint title="Main claim (sample)">
Self-supervised pre-training on multi-exposure data yields representations that transfer more reliably to HDR downstream tasks than ImageNet-pretrained baselines, especially when fine-tuning data is scarce.
</KeyPoint>

## Contributions

1. A self-supervised pre-training strategy based on exposure-contrastive learning.
2. A benchmark suite for evaluating HDR representation transfer.
3. Empirical analysis of the relationship between exposure diversity in pre-training data and downstream task performance.
