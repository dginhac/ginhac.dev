## Abstract

We study the deployment of compact object detection models on embedded vision systems with sub-watt power budgets. Starting from a lightweight backbone, we apply a combination of structured pruning, 8-bit post-training quantization, and operator fusion to produce a model that meets real-time latency constraints on a representative edge platform.

We evaluate the resulting system on a standard detection benchmark and a custom dataset of industrial inspection scenes. Our compressed model retains 97% of the full-precision mAP while reducing inference time by 4.2× and peak memory by 3.8×.



* **Latency**: 24 ms per frame at 640×480 resolution on the target platform.
* **mAP**: 97% of the uncompressed baseline on the standard evaluation set.
* **Memory**: 3.8× reduction in peak activation memory.
